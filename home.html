Women Empowerment in the Face of Crime

Empowering Women against Crime at the intersection of gender, justice, and safety is a critical question Empowering women means giving them the tools, resources and life-skills to be able to self direct their lives and make wise choices. Women burden high levels of vulnerabilities- to physical violence, sexual assault and psychological abuse especially in contexts where criminal acts are common. These threats deprive them of their safety and limit them in participating socially, economically, and politically.

Legal reforms, educational outreach, economic investments and social support systems are effective strategies with which women may be empowered. Society can minimize the consequences of crimes committed against women by teaching women self-defense, educating them about their rights and providing safe spaces. Creating a workplace of respect and equality also encourages the scrutiny of absurd gender- and race-based misconceptions.
