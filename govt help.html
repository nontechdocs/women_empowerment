Help From Government

The Indian government has implemented various safety measures and legal rights to protect women from crimes and ensure their safety and well-being. Here are some of the key measures and legal provisions:

Safety Measures
1. 1. Women Helpline Numbers

• National Helpline: The national helpline number 181 provides emergency assistance to women facing violence or harassment.

• Police Helpline: The police helpline number 100 is available for immediate assistance in emergencies.

2. One-Stop Centers (OSCs)

• Support Services: OSCs provide support and assistance to women affected by violence, including medical aid, police assistance, legal aid, counseling, and temporary shelter.

3. Mobile Apps and Online Platforms

• Himmat App: Launched by the Delhi Police, this app allows women to send distress signals along with their location to the police control room.

• My Safetipin: A mobile app that provides safety scores for areas based on factors like lighting, public transport, and crowd density, helping women make informed decisions about their safety.

• SOS: A mobile app that provides safety for women and its application is very easy to use by anyone.

4. Self-Defense Training Programs

• Initiatives by Police Departments: Various police departments across the country organize self-defense training programs for women and girls to empower them with skills to protect themselves.

5. Safe City Projects

• ,b>Infrastructure Improvements: The government has initiated Safe City projects in several cities to improve infrastructure, such as better street lighting, CCTV surveillance, and women-only police stations.

Legal Rights and Provisions
1. The Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2013

• Stricter Punishments: This amendment introduced stricter punishments for sexual offenses, including rape, sexual harassment, acid attacks, and stalking.

• New Offenses: The act defined new offenses such as voyeurism and stalking and prescribed stringent punishments for them.

2. Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005

• Protection Orders: Provides legal protection to women from domestic violence and allows them to seek protection orders, residence orders, and monetary relief.• Support Services: The act mandates the appointment of Protection Officers and the establishment of support services such as shelters and medical facilities.

3. The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013

• Internal Complaints Committee (ICC): Mandates the establishment of an ICC in every workplace to address complaints of sexual harassment.

• Employer Responsibilities: Employers are required to create a safe working environment and conduct awareness programs on sexual harassment.

4. The Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961

• Ban on Dowry: Prohibits the giving or taking of dowry and prescribes penalties for those engaging in such practices.

• Protection Measures: Provides for the protection of women from dowry-related harassment and violence.

5. The Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956

• Anti-Trafficking Measures: Aims to prevent trafficking for commercial sexual exploitation and provides for the rescue and rehabilitation of victims.

6. The Protection of Children from Sexual Offenses (POCSO) Act, 2012

• Protection for Minors: Provides legal protection to children from sexual abuse and exploitation, with stringent penalties for offenders.

• Child-Friendly Procedures: Ensures child-friendly procedures for reporting, recording of evidence, investigation, and trial of offenses.

7. The Indecent Representation of Women (Prohibition) Act, 1986

• Prohibition of Indecent Representation: Prohibits the indecent representation of women through advertisements, publications, and other forms of media.

Sections according to Indian Penal Code
IPC Section 498A

Offense: Husband or relative of husband of a woman subjecting her to cruelty. Punishment: Imprisonment for up to 3 years and a fine.

IPC Section 494

Offense: Marrying again during the lifetime of a husband or wife (bigamy). Punishment: Imprisonment for up to 7 years and a fine.

IPC Section 304B

Offense: Dowry death. Punishment: Imprisonment for a minimum of 7 years, which may extend to life imprisonment.

IPC Section 366

Offense: Kidnapping, abducting, or inducing a woman to compel her marriage, etc.

Punishment: Imprisonment for up to 10 years and a fine.

IPC Section 354A to 354F

354A: Sexual harassment and punishment for sexual harassment.

• Punishment: Imprisonment for up to 3 years or with a fine, or both for specific acts.

354B: Assault or use of criminal force to woman with intent to disrobe.

• Punishment: Imprisonment for not less than 3 years but which may extend to 7 years, and a fine.

354C: Voyeurism.

• Punishment: Imprisonment for 1 to 3 years for the first conviction and 3 to 7 years for a subsequent conviction, plus a fine.

354D: Stalking.

• Punishment: Imprisonment for up to 3 years for the first conviction and up to 5 years for a subsequent conviction, plus a fine.

354E to 354F: Specific newer provisions addressing more detailed acts of harassment or assault.

IPC Section 506

Offense: Criminal intimidation. Punishment: Imprisonment for up to 2 years, or with a fine, or both; if the threat is to cause death or grievous hurt, etc., imprisonment for up to 7 years.

IPC Section 376A to 376D

376A: Causing death or resulting in a persistent vegetative state of the victim.

• Punishment: Rigorous imprisonment for 20 years to life imprisonment, or death.

376B: Sexual intercourse by husband upon his wife during separation.

• Punishment: Imprisonment for 2 to 7 years and a fine.

376C: Sexual intercourse by a person in authority.

• Punishment: Imprisonment for 5 to 10 years and a fine.

376D: Gang rape.

• Punishment: Imprisonment for not less than 20 years to life imprisonment and a fine.

IPC Section 420

Offense: Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property. Punishment: Imprisonment for up to 7 years and a fine.

IPC Section 370

Offense: Trafficking of persons. Punishment: Rigorous imprisonment for 7 years to 10 years and a fine. If the victim is a minor, the punishment increases significantly.

Conclusion

The Indian government has established a comprehensive framework of safety measures and legal rights to protect women from various forms of crimes and violence. These initiatives aim to create a safer environment for women, empower them with knowledge and skills, and ensure their access to justice and support services. By continually enhancing these measures and raising awareness, the government strives to improve the safety and well-being of women across the country.
